Pattern Recognition

A discovered attack where the newly revealed threat is check. The moving piece can capture, threaten, or wreak havoc anywhere — the king must answer the check first. Discovered Check is a pattern you want to recognise before calculation becomes heavy. The visual clue matters first, then the forcing move, then the final conversion. Training it means learning both the attacking idea and the defensive escape attempts.

Effect: Check + free move

Trigger Signs

  • The king has limited escape squares.
  • A valuable piece is lined up with a weaker defender or the king.
  • One defender is protecting too many targets.
  • A forcing move gives check, wins tempo, or creates two threats at once.

Calculation Routine

  • Start with checks, then captures, then threats.
  • Name the target before choosing the move.
  • Look for the defender's best response, not the response you hope for.
  • After the tactic works, calculate one extra move to make sure the gain cannot be returned.

How Players Miss It

Most missed tactics are not calculation failures. They are attention failures. The position contains a loose piece, trapped king, overloaded defender, or hidden line, but the player never asks which piece has no safe square.

Training Drill

Find five puzzle examples of Discovered Check. For each one, write the trigger sign in words before solving. The habit of naming the pattern is what makes it appear faster in real games.

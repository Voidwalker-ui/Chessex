Zwischenzug: The In-Between Move

The zwischenzug (German: 'in-between move') is the art of inserting a forcing move into what looks like a forced sequence, changing the outcome. It is one of the most useful and underappreciated tactical concepts at club level.

Also known as: intermezzoKey idea: interrupt expected sequence with a stronger threatDifficulty: Intermediate
♚ ♙ ♙ ♙ ♕ ♔

White to move. The obvious recapture or defensive move may not be best — a forcing intermezzo can completely change the evaluation.

What it is

When a position appears to demand an automatic recapture or defensive move, the zwischenzug is the move that says: 'not yet.' Instead of the expected response, the player makes a different, more powerful move first — usually a check, threat to a major piece, or a decisive positional gain — and only then, if at all, makes the 'obligatory' move.

The opponent, forced to respond to the new threat, loses the chance to complete what they planned.

A simple example

White plays Nxe5, attacking a pawn. Black plans to recapture Nxe5. But White has a zwischenzug: before Black captures, White plays Bxf7+ (check). The King must move. Now White recaptures on e5 and is ahead in material.

How to spot it

  • Identify sequences that look automatic — recaptures, forced trades.
  • Before completing the sequence, ask: is there a check, a double attack, or a winning threat I can insert first?
  • Check if the in-between move forces a response that improves your final position.
  • The zwischenzug works because the opponent cannot ignore the intermezzo threat.

The discipline

Before every recapture, pause and ask: is there something better to do first? This habit alone will save you from missing zwischenzugs constantly.

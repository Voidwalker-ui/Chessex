Newbie · The Pieces

The Queen

The Queen is the most powerful piece on the board. It combines the reach of the Rook and the Bishop into one piece, able to control entire diagonals, files, and ranks in a single move.

Movement: any number of squares — rank, file, or diagonalPoint value: ~9 pointsStarting square: d1 (White), d8 (Black)
♕

The Queen (♕) on d5. Every highlighted square is reachable in one move — along both diagonals, the entire rank, and the entire file. From a central square it controls 27 of the board's 64 squares.

How the Queen moves

The Queen can move any number of squares along a rank, file, or diagonal — in any of eight directions — as long as no piece blocks the path. It cannot jump over pieces. From a central square, the Queen covers more squares than any other piece.

Combining Rook and Bishop

Think of the Queen as a Rook and Bishop fused together. The Rook moves along ranks and files; the Bishop moves along diagonals. The Queen does both. This makes it the dominant piece in close-range attacks and long-range control alike.

When to use the Queen

The Queen is dangerous early, but bringing it out too soon invites attacks that waste tempo. A common beginner mistake is 'queen raids' — moving the Queen to grab pawns while the opponent develops pieces and gains a lead in activity.

The Queen is most effective when it has support from other pieces and enough space to retreat safely. Use it to coordinate attacks, not as a lone raider.

Exchanges

Losing the Queen for anything less than the opposing Queen is almost always catastrophic. A Queen-vs-three-minor-pieces trade is roughly even in material but often disadvantageous in practice — three coordinated minor pieces can be formidable and harder to attack. Treat the Queen as precious.

Golden rule

The Queen is strongest when working alongside other pieces. Alone on the board, it can be chased, trapped, or sacrificed against. Coordinate.

Position Goal

A passed pawn on the flank acts as a decoy, drawing the enemy king away from the main battle while your own king invades on the other side. A strategically decisive weapon at all levels. Technique Checklist

  • Activate the king before calculating pawn races.
  • Identify the key squares, checking distance and opposition before pushing.
  • Ask what the defender is threatening on every move.
  • Convert slowly: remove counterplay first, then advance the decisive pawn or piece.

Defensive Resources

The weaker side is not only waiting. In this family of endgames the defender looks for stalemate tricks, checking distance, fortress construction, and tempo changes. A winning position can become drawn if the stronger side pushes too early.

  • Check from the side or behind when possible.
  • Trade pawns, not pieces, if the trade removes the winning passer.
  • Keep the king near the promotion square or blockade square.
  • Use zugzwang only after confirming the opponent has no waiting move.

Most Common Error

The usual mistake is treating the endgame as obvious because there are few pieces. Fewer pieces make every tempo more important. Before moving, count the shortest route for each king and decide whether the position is won, drawn, or still needs a preparatory move.

Practice Drill

Set up the diagram and play it once from the stronger side and once from the defending side. If you cannot explain the result without moving the pieces, repeat the position until the route becomes visual.
